Introduction into Wireshark

So what is wireshark anyways?



Wireshark can do many things when it comes to analyzing network traffic. But here's a few that will get you interested about learning about Wireshark.
  • If you're studying for your Network+ exam that wireshark is a great way to get some practical experience working with Wireshark. So download the app, and what are are you waiting for?!
  • If you want to find out if anyone is snooping your network you can easily scan your entire network to find out is eavesdropping on your home network.
  • Analyze individual packets and frames and deconstruct them to potential unencrypt HTTPS traffic, find out if any nodes in your system is taking up ressources in your network.

Click here to navigate to the website to download the application and scroll and find excellent tutorials on how to learn Wireshark and a great community of fellow networking gurus to guide you through the ocean that is Wireshark. Including their own YouTube channel!

How To Download Wireshark


Navigate to the website and hover over "Get Acquainted and click "Download" Get the latest version and you're off!!! Once you're able to open the application, it will ask you which network you want to scan. And once you click your ethernet or wireless adapter it will start scanning right away!

When I finished downloading the virtual machine, Kali did not want to boot, it simply gave me a black screen and a flashing cursor. To fix this I had to do a little research online but I finally got it. I started by making sure Virtualization was enabled on my Windows machine, then I reinstalled kali linux from the .iso file. Then I went into the command prompt using administrator privileges and entered the following: bcdedit /set hypervisorlaunchtype off Reboot your computer and try to boot Kali from the hypervisor. This is a common issue with lots of help online. Windows has its own hypervisor configuration that is enabled at bootup, but Virtual Box is in conflict with this so it is best to turn it off.
